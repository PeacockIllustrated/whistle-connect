Part One — The Shape Of It
03
The big picture

One app. Two sides of the pitch.
Money safely in the middle.

Whistle Connect is a booking platform that connects grassroots football coaches who need a referee with local, verified referees who are available. The app handles the four things that used to live in WhatsApp threads, texts, and phone calls: finding a ref, agreeing a price, paying them, and staying in touch on match day.

A coach creates a booking for a fixture, searches nearby referees, and sends a priced offer. A referee reviews the offer in their inbox and either accepts or declines. The moment they accept, money is held in escrow, an assignment is locked in, and a private thread opens so both parties can sort the final details.

After the match, either party marks it complete. Escrow is released into the referee's wallet. They withdraw to their bank when they like. An admin sits above all of this, verifying referees with the FA, handling disputes, and tuning platform settings.

The whole design goal: no money moves between strangers without the platform in the middle, no fixture goes uncovered because a coach couldn't find the right person, no referee turns up to a match they thought was cancelled.

Coaches get referees found and confirmed in minutes. Referees get fair, guaranteed pay. Admin gets visibility and compliance. Nobody has to trust a stranger on a WhatsApp thread with their Saturday morning.

Personas

Three roles. One platform.

Every user on Whistle Connect is exactly one of these. The role is set at signup and controls which screens they see and which actions they can take. When debugging, "what role is this user?" is always the first question.

Role 01
The Coach
Organises matches and pays for referees. Typically a volunteer coach, club secretary, or fixture secretary. Budgets from a club or team wallet.
Home screen shows
  • Upcoming bookings needing a ref
  • Offers waiting on acceptance
  • Recently completed matches
  • Wallet balance and escrow held
Unique powers
  • Create and edit bookings
  • Search and filter referees
  • Send priced offers
  • Trigger an SOS emergency broadcast
  • Top up and spend from the wallet
  • Raise a dispute before release
Role 02
The Referee
Officiates matches and gets paid per booking. Must be FA-verified to appear in searches; often needs a DBS check for youth games.
Home screen shows
  • Upcoming confirmed assignments
  • Pending offers in their inbox
  • Match feed of nearby bookings
  • Earnings so far this season
Unique powers
  • Flip the master availability toggle
  • Set weekly and date-specific slots
  • Control travel radius and county
  • Opt in to central venue matches
  • Accept, decline, or counter offers
  • Withdraw earned balance to a bank
Role 03
The Admin
Keeps the platform honest. Verifies referees with the FA, resolves disputed payments, configures platform-wide settings.
Home screen shows
  • Pending FA verification queue
  • Unverified referees still in signup
  • Bookings this month · SOS count
  • Overall user and activity stats
Unique powers
  • Approve or reject FA verifications
  • Ban or reinstate accounts
  • Resolve disputes and split funds
  • Set travel cost per km platform-wide
  • View all users on a moderation map
  • Impersonate for live support
If this person can't do what they expect, nine times out of ten they're logged in as the wrong role. A "Test Coach" with role = coach cannot set availability. A "Test Referee" accidentally seeded as a coach will never appear in searches. Check the profiles.role column first.

Part One — The Coach's Journey
05
User flow · Coach

From fixture in the diary
to referee in the middle.

Six steps, front to back. This is the spine of the product for 90% of sessions. Memorising it makes every support conversation, demo, and roadmap question easier.

1
Create booking

Coach fills date, kickoff, postcode, age group, format, budget. Postcode is geocoded to lat/long.

2
Search referees

System filters available refs by radius, county, availability, DBS, and central-venue opt-in.

3
Send offers

Coach proposes match fee. Travel cost added automatically. Offers sent to one or more refs.

4
Acceptance

First referee to accept wins. Escrow held. Competing offers auto-withdrawn. Thread opens.

5
Match day

Both sides chat in the thread. Kickoff happens. Either party marks the match complete.

6
Payout

Escrow released to referee. Coach's wallet reconciled. Transaction logged immutably.

When it goes sideways

  • No referees found. Most common cause is the coach's postcode didn't geocode, so the spatial search fell back to a county-name exact match that doesn't line up.
  • Offer fails to send. Rate-limited (too many offers in a short window) or the coach's wallet hasn't got the balance to cover the potential escrow hold.
  • Referee pulls out. Booking reverts to pending, escrow refunded, coach notified. Coach can re-search and re-offer without re-creating the booking.
  • Match cancelled last minute. Referee SOS broadcast as a high-urgency push notification to every available ref in radius. First to express interest gets auto-assigned.

The single rule to remember

Money does not leave the coach's wallet until a referee accepts. Not when the booking is created. Not when the offer is sent. Only on acceptance. That's why a coach can have ten offers out with one fixture's worth of budget — whichever ref accepts first wins, and the rest silently close.

Offers are non-binding promises. Acceptance is the binding moment. Every cash movement is anchored there.

Part One — The Referee's Journey
06
User flow · Referee

From signup to payday.

A referee's job on the app is essentially to stay findable, respond to offers quickly, and get paid. The product is designed so a new ref can get useful within their first ten minutes: register, get FA-verified, set availability, toggle the big green switch, and wait for offers.

1
Register & FA

Pick role = Referee, enter FA number. Admin verifies via the FA; badge appears once approved.

2
Set availability

Weekly slots (e.g. Sat 9–5), or specific dates. Master toggle must be on.

3
Get offered

Offers appear in /app/offers with the match fee and travel cost clearly broken out.

4
Accept / decline

Acceptance is atomic — money held, assignment locked, thread opens, other offers withdrawn.

5
Match & payout

Officiate. Mark complete. Escrow lands in wallet. Withdraw to bank via Stripe Connect.

The accept button can fail with Insufficient funds — that's not the referee's wallet, it's the coach's. The atomic acceptance needs the coach to have enough balance to cover match fee plus travel.

The five-minute onboarding

  1. Register with role = Referee. FA number goes in during signup.
  2. Wait for FA verification from admin — usually same-day.
  3. Set county and home postcode on the profile so coordinates get stored.
  4. Flip the master availability toggle to green.
  5. Add weekly slots for the days you typically officiate.
Referee home · /app

Why refs become invisible

The master availability toggle defaults off on older accounts. That one switch has historically caused more "my ref isn't showing up" tickets than anything else combined. It's now on by default, red when off, and asks for confirmation before going off.

Part Two — Booking Lifecycle
07
Workflow · Booking states

Every booking rides one of two rails.

A booking is born as a pending fixture and ends either completed or cancelled. Nothing skips states. Every status transition is logged and auditable. If you know the state, you know who's waiting on whom.

draft

Coach saving a booking without posting it yet.

pending

Live and searchable. Coach is looking for a ref.

offered

At least one offer has been sent. Awaiting response.

confirmed

Referee accepted. Money in escrow. Thread open.

completed

Match played. Escrow released to referee.

↓ or at any point ↓
cancelled

Coach or ref pulled out. Escrow refunded if held.

The transitions that trigger money

offered → confirmed holds escrow out of the coach's balance.
confirmed → completed releases escrow into the referee's balance.
anything → cancelled returns any escrow to the coach.

The transitions that trigger notifications

Every state change notifies the other party via in-app, web push, and (for mobile app users) native push. Cancellations always route through both channels because they're time-sensitive.

The transitions that affect the thread

The thread only exists from confirmed onwards. A booking going confirmed → cancelled keeps the thread alive as a record; a pending → cancelled never had one in the first place.

The transitions that touch availability

When a referee accepts, their conflicting date-specific slot for that match date is automatically removed so they can't be double-booked. Weekly recurring slots are untouched.

Part Two — Offer Lifecycle
08
Workflow · Offer states

One booking. Many offers.
One winner.

A booking usually has multiple offers out at once. Each one is its own tiny negotiation. The first referee to accept wins the assignment and every other offer is silently withdrawn. The referees who weren't quick enough get a polite in-app notice.

draft → sent → accepted priced → accepted
sent → declined sent → withdrawn
  • sent — the offer is live in the referee's inbox. Clock is ticking.
  • accepted_priced — referee has confirmed the price; transient state during the atomic confirmation.
  • accepted — terminal success. Booking moves to confirmed.
  • declined — referee said no. Coach can re-offer elsewhere.
  • withdrawn — another offer on the same booking won, or the coach cancelled.

The atomic accept

Clicking "Accept" does not just update one row. In one indivisible database transaction the system: updates the offer status, holds the escrow, creates the assignment, moves the booking to confirmed, withdraws every competing offer on that booking, creates a messaging thread with both participants, posts a system message, removes the ref's conflicting date-specific slot, and fires a notification to the coach. If any single step fails, none of them happen.

This is why "I accepted but nothing happened" is almost impossible in normal operation. Either it all happens, or none of it does and a clear error is shown.

Fairness by design

Competing offers don't require the referee to "refuse" them — they close automatically. A ref never has to feel guilty or leave someone hanging.

Part Two — Matching Logic
09
The seven filters

Why a referee appears
(or doesn't) in a search.

A coach's search is a funnel. A referee has to pass every single filter to make the list. Miss one and they're invisible — no error, no warning, just absent. This page is the support team's cheat sheet for "my ref isn't showing up."

1
The referee has a referee_profiles row.

No row = invisible. Normally created automatically on signup; occasionally missing if the signup trigger failed. The fix is to backfill the row.

2
Master availability toggle is on.

The big green/red switch on /app/availability. Defaults on now. Being off wipes them from every search regardless of everything else.

3
Availability window covers the kickoff.

A date-specific or weekly slot that brackets the match time. If they only set 14:30–16:30 and kickoff is 14:00, they fail this — unless the master toggle rescues them.

4
Location fits the booking.

Spatial: within 50 km of the booking's postcode. Or fallback: county name matches exactly. A ref with no stored lat/long silently drops out of spatial searches and has to rely on the county fallback.

5
No clashing confirmed booking that day.

A ref already assigned to a confirmed match on the same date is excluded. They can't be double-booked.

6
Passes the DBS rule.

If the booking's age group is U16 or younger, dbs_status = 'verified' is required. Otherwise ignored.

7
Passes the central-venue rule.

If the booking is type central (a multi-game central-venue event), the referee must have ticked Central Venue Opt-In. Individual bookings ignore this flag.

Admin "verified" is not a filter on the search. Being FA-verified only unlocks the green badge shown to coaches; it doesn't affect visibility. We've seen tickets where ops assumes verification = visibility and loses half a day chasing the wrong thing.

Part Two — Referee's Self-Check
10
Am I findable?

The referee's eight-step
visibility checklist.

If a ref calls support saying "no one's booking me," this is the script. Walk through it with them in order. By step three or four, you'll usually have found it.

  1. Master toggle is green. Top of /app/availability. Red means you're invisible. Also sets the tab colour for instant recognition.
  2. County is picked from the dropdown in profile. This powers the fallback search when coordinates are unavailable.
  3. Home postcode is saved. Geocoding turns this into lat/long automatically. Without it, spatial searches can't place you.
  4. Travel radius is set (default 15 km, max 50). Anything beyond this and you won't see the booking in your match feed.
  5. Slots cover when you can officiate — either weekly recurring (e.g. Saturdays 10–18) or a specific date, or both.
  6. Central venue opt-in is ticked if you want those multi-game central events in your search results.
  7. FA verification is approved. Shows as a badge. Admin does this after you register. Doesn't block search, but it builds trust.
  8. DBS status is verified if you want to officiate youth matches. Required for U16 and under.
Availability screen · /app/availability

The most common miss

By a wide margin: step one. The master toggle was off. Either never turned on, or accidentally switched off and left. That's why the app now defaults it on, paints it red when off, and asks for confirmation before going off.

Part Three — Messaging Model
11
Messaging

A thread is a record of a
confirmed booking.

There is no general chat. No friend lists. No DMs. Every thread is tied to exactly one confirmed booking, has exactly two participants (coach + referee), and is born the moment the referee accepts the offer.

What a thread looks like

Booking confirmed. Use chat to finalise details. Kickoff 14:00 at Marsden FC — Saturday 27th April.
Thanks for taking this! Changing rooms are down the side of the clubhouse. Reach me on 07xxx if anything comes up.
No problem — I'll be there 30 mins before. Can you confirm it's 9v9, U12?
Yes, 9v9 U12, two halves of 30. See you there.

Messages are of kind user or system. System messages are auto-generated (like the confirmation) and styled distinctly so there's no confusion about who "said" what.

The visibility rules

  • Only the two participants can read a thread. Coach sees their threads, referee sees theirs, admin does not read private threads.
  • Unread badges come from each participant's last read at timestamp. When you open the thread, it bumps. Anything after that is "unread."
  • Threads outlive cancellations. Once born, a thread stays visible for audit even if the booking later cancels.
  • Messages survive account deletion. Deleted users show as anonymised, but historical messages persist for FA audit compliance.
Seeing a thread in your inbox is a strong signal: a booking has been confirmed. If you're seeing threads but no confirmed bookings in reports, something is wrong with the status reporting, not with the messaging.

Why no general chat

Scope discipline. Messaging exists for match-day coordination, not social networking. Every thread has a clear business purpose; every thread is auditable. This keeps support, safeguarding, and moderation simple.

Part Three — Notifications
12
Notifications

One event. Four channels.
One user sees exactly one.

Every important action — a new offer, an accept, a cancel, a new message — triggers a single notification event. That event fans out to multiple channels based on what the user has enabled. The user experiences it as one gentle nudge, not four.

One event fires.

e.g. "Booking accepted" or "New message in thread" from a server action or database trigger.

IN
In-app notification

A row in the notifications table. Shown in the bell/sidebar whenever the user is on the site.

WEB
Web push

VAPID-signed browser notification. Works even when the tab is closed if the user has granted permission.

APP
Native push (Capacitor)

Firebase Cloud Messaging to the iOS/Android app. Phone vibrates, app badge updates.

SOS
High-priority push

SOS bookings use the "high" urgency flag so devices bypass Do Not Disturb and ring through.

When notifications fire

Offer sent · offer declined · offer accepted · booking cancelled · new message · match completed · availability auto-removed · SOS broadcast

Where subscriptions live

The push_subscriptions table holds each user's web and Firebase tokens. Registered on first app launch when permission is granted.

Emails too

Signup confirmation, password reset, and invite emails run through Supabase Auth templates — not the in-app notification pipeline.

If a user says "I'm not getting notifications," ask which channel they expected. Web push needs explicit browser permission. Native push needs the mobile app installed. In-app needs them on the site. These are three separate things to troubleshoot.

Part Three — Wallet & Escrow
13
Money flow

Money flows left to right,
and never in a hurry.

Every user has one wallet with two pots: balance (available to spend or withdraw) and escrow (held against a confirmed booking). Money moves between pots and between users, always via the platform, always with an immutable audit row.

Step 1 · Top-up
Coach wallet
Stripe Checkout. Coach adds balance. Logged as top_up.
→
On referee accept
Step 2 · Escrow
Held in escrow
Match fee + travel. Locked until match completes or cancels.
→
On match complete
Step 3 · Release
Referee wallet
Balance increases. Withdraws to bank via Stripe Connect.

Match fee

Coach's own number. Whatever they've agreed with the ref or what their county standard is.

Travel cost

Distance in km × admin rate (default 28p/km). Added transparently to every offer.

Escrow hold

Happens the moment the ref accepts. Fails cleanly if the coach is short, with no partial state.

Disputes

Either party can raise one before release. Admin resolves and decides how to split.

The ledger is immutable.

Every movement — top-up, escrow hold, escrow release, withdrawal, refund — is a row in wallet_transactions with a type and a direction (credit or debit). Nothing is ever edited or deleted. This is what makes reconciliation, auditing, and dispute resolution tractable.

Part Three — The Admin View
14
Admin

The platform's safety net.

Admin's core job is making sure the referees on the platform are real, the payments move cleanly, and the bad actors don't stay. Everything else — maps, reports, settings — is instrumentation.

FA verification — the daily job

  1. Queue opens at /app/admin/verification with referees awaiting check.
  2. Admin runs the external FA lookup using the referee's FA number and county.
  3. FA returns verified or rejected; admin clicks the matching button.
  4. Referee's profile badge updates instantly; they get an in-app + email notice.

Other admin controls

  • Dispute resolution. Open disputes queue shows contested bookings; admin reads both sides and resolves in favour of coach, referee, or a split.
  • Account moderation. Ban, reinstate, or impersonate for live support. Impersonation is logged.
  • Platform settings. Travel rate per km, notification defaults, rate limits — all tuneable from one page.
  • Moderation map. All users plotted. Useful for spotting unusual patterns or impersonation attempts.

Reference · Tables

Every table. One sentence each.

When you hear a table name in a standup or on a ticket, you'll know what it is. Colour-coded by concern: people, bookings, messaging, money, operations.

profiles

The core user account — name, role (coach/ref/admin), avatar, postcode, coordinates.

referee_profiles

Extra data that only matters for referees — FA ID, level, DBS, travel radius, the master available toggle.

referee_availability

Recurring weekly slots — which days and times a ref is generally free.

One-off slots for specific dates. Overrides or supplements the weekly pattern.

bookings

A fixture the coach needs officiated — date, time, location, age group, format, status.

booking_offers

A priced proposal from coach to ref. Tracks whether it was accepted, declined, or withdrawn.

booking_assignments

The confirmed pairing. One per booking. Created the moment a ref accepts.

threads

The chat container. One thread per confirmed booking. Two participants.

thread_participants

Who's in a thread, plus their last-read timestamp for unread counts.

messages

Individual chat messages. Kind is either user (typed) or system (auto).

notifications

The bell-icon feed. Rows per user with a type, title, link, read-at.

push_subscriptions

Web push and Firebase tokens. Lets the server reach the device.

wallets

wallet_transactions

travel_expenses

Per-booking travel breakdown — distance, rate, computed cost.

disputes

Admin-tunable key/value pairs — e.g. travel cost per km.

club_pools

Shared club-level wallets for teams pooling their referee budget.

club_pool_members

Who can draw from a club pool and in what capacity.

audit_log

Immutable record of admin actions — verifications, bans, dispute resolutions.

Part Four — The Non-Obvious Connections
17
The glue nobody talks about

Seven things that will save
you half a day of debugging.

Geocoding is the glue.
Postcodes are turned into lat/long on both bookings and profiles. Without coordinates on either side, the spatial search silently falls back to county-name exact match. That's case-sensitive and fragile. When matching feels broken, check coordinates first.
Threads are born from payment acceptance.
No thread exists until a referee accepts. So seeing a thread in your inbox is a strong positive signal: the booking did confirm. If the status dashboard disagrees, trust the threads.
Acceptance is one atomic action.
Money hold, assignment creation, status change, competing-offer withdrawal, thread creation, slot cleanup, and coach notification all happen inside one database transaction. It all commits or none of it does. You never see half-finished state.
Escrow is held on accept, not on offer.
A coach who sends five offers is not locking up 5× the cash. Only the accepted offer holds money. This is why acceptance can fail with Insufficient funds when the offer itself sent fine — the balance check is at the acceptance moment.
SOS uses a different push priority.
Regular notifications fire at "normal" urgency. SOS broadcasts use "high" so devices bypass Do Not Disturb and actually ring. Don't be surprised if a user reports "my app notifications are silent except SOS."
Auto-slot-cleanup is per-date only.
When a ref accepts, their conflicting date-specific slot for that match date is auto-deleted. Their weekly recurring pattern is not touched. A ref who sets weekly Saturdays 10–18 will keep showing as available for every other Saturday.
"Verified" ≠ "Findable".
Admin verification shows the badge to coaches, but it is not a filter on search results. A ref can be verified and still invisible because their master availability toggle is off. Don't confuse the two.
When a support ticket comes in, walk through these seven in order. Nine times out of ten, the answer is on this page.
