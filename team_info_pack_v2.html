01 · The Shape— The three personas
WC · V2 · 0404
Who uses it
Three roles.
Different screens.
Some overlap.

Every user is one of these three — set at signup. The role decides which pages they can open and which buttons they can press. Where the circles overlap, the feature is shared: messages, a wallet, a profile, a map. Where they don't, it's exclusive.

01 COACH
Organises fixtures, pays referees.
Create bookings. Send offers. Trigger SOS.
02 REFEREE
Officiates matches, gets paid.
Set availability. Accept offers. Withdraw.
03 ADMIN
Keeps the platform honest.
Verify FA. Resolve disputes. Tune.
Rule of thumb
When a user can't do what they expect, check profiles.role first. Role mismatches are the #1 cause of "it's broken" that isn't.
01 · The Shape— Two journeys, one moment
WC · V2 · 0505
Side by side
Two people meet at accept.

The coach's journey and the referee's journey run on parallel rails. They cross once — at the moment the ref taps accept. Everything before is parallel, everything after is shared.

Before accept
Independent. Coach is hunting, ref is waiting. No money moved. No commitment.
At accept
Everything happens at once, atomically. Half-states are impossible.
After accept
Shared. Both parties have a thread, a commitment, and a kickoff to get to.

02 · Workflows— An offer's life
WC · V2 · 0707
An offer's life
Many offers out. One winner. The rest close on their own.

A coach can send the same booking out to multiple refs at once. The first ref to accept wins. Every other offer for that booking gets quietly marked withdrawn — nobody has to say no. It's a race, but a polite one.

accepted_priced
A transient state only ever seen inside a database transaction. If you see it at rest, alert engineering.
accepted
Terminal success. Booking flips to confirmed, escrow holds, thread opens.
withdrawn
Silent, polite closure. Ref doesn't act. App marks it gone with a "went to another ref" note.

03 · Connections— Notification fan-out
WC · V2 · 1111
One event, many channels
A single event
reaches a user
however they'll listen.

Every meaningful action — an offer sent, an accept, a new message — fires one server event. That event fans out to whichever channels the user has enabled. They experience one nudge, not four.

When a user says "I'm not getting notifications…"
…first ask which channel they expected. Web push needs browser permission. Native push needs the mobile app installed. In-app needs them on the site. These are three separate troubleshoots.
Triggers fire on
offer sent · accepted · declined · cancelled · new message · match complete · SOS broadcast
Subscriptions stored in
push_subscriptions · VAPID for web · Firebase tokens for native
03 · Connections— The money flow
WC · V2 · 1212
How money moves
Three pots. One direction. One ledger.

Every user's wallet is two pots: balance (spendable) and escrow (locked). Money moves left to right — coach wallet, through a shared escrow while the match is live, out to the referee's wallet on completion. Every movement writes an immutable row to the ledger.

Match fee
Coach's proposed number. Usually county standard.
Travel
Distance × admin rate (default 28p/km). Transparent on every offer.
Dispute window
Either party can raise before release; admin resolves the split.
Payout
Ref withdraws via Stripe Connect to their bank.

04 · Reference— The hidden wiring
WC · V2 · 1616
Seven things nobody writes down
The glue behind the glue.

The small quirks and invisible rules that save half a day of debugging when you know them. Tattoo this somewhere the support team can find it.

01 · GEOCODING

Postcodes become coordinates — or matching silently breaks.

Both bookings and profiles store lat/long. Without them, search falls back to exact-match county names. Case-sensitive and fragile.
02 · THREADS

A thread existing is a strong "booking confirmed" signal.

No thread is ever created for a pending or offered booking. If your dashboard disagrees, trust the threads.
03 · ATOMIC ACCEPT

All seven side-effects commit together — or none do.

Hold, assign, status, withdraw siblings, open thread, clean slot, notify. One transaction. You never see half-finished state.
04 · ESCROW TIMING

Money is held on accept, not on offer.

Coaches can send ten offers with one fixture's worth of cash. Only the winning acceptance pulls money from balance.
05 · SOS URGENCY

SOS uses a different push priority.

High urgency flag bypasses Do Not Disturb. "My app is silent" is expected behaviour outside of an SOS.
06 · SLOT CLEANUP

Accepting auto-removes the date-specific slot only.

Weekly recurring patterns stay intact. A ref with "every Saturday 10-18" keeps showing for the next Saturday.
07 · THE BIG ONE

"Verified" ≠ "Findable".

Admin verification shows a badge to coaches, but it is not a filter on search results. A ref can be FA-verified and still invisible because their master availability toggle is off. Don't waste time chasing verification when the symptom is "they're not showing up." Check the toggle first.

WC · V2 · 1919
Key terms, one line each
Glossary.
FA
The Football Association — the referee's governing body. FA verification is the admin step that confirms a registered number.
DBS
Disclosure & Barring Service check. Required to officiate youth matches (U16 and under).
ESCROW
The locked pot holding a coach's money while a booking is live. Released on completion; refunded on cancel.
SOS
Emergency booking flow. Higher-urgency notifications go to every available ref in radius.
THREAD
A confirmed booking's chat. Born at accept, two participants, permanent record.
OFFER
A priced invitation from a coach to a specific referee. Non-binding until accepted.
ASSIGNMENT
The confirmed pairing of a referee to a booking. One per booking. Created atomically.
CENTRAL VENUE
A multi-game event at one location. Separate opt-in flag on referee profiles.
TRAVEL COST
Distance × admin rate (default 28p/km). Added transparently to every offer.
MASTER TOGGLE
The big green/red switch on a referee's availability page. Off = invisible. On = findable.
PARTICIPANT
A user in a thread. Two per thread, never more, never fewer.
LEDGER
Immutable log of every money movement. Never edited, never deleted. Audit-friendly by design.
